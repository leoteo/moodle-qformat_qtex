\question
    {Which of the following statements are correct?}
        \true{$i^2 = -1$}
        \true{$\frac 1i = -i$}
        \true{$i^3 = -i$}
        \true{$i^{17} = i$}
        \false{None.}    
        \explanation{For the imaginary unit $i$ holds $i^2 = -1$,
            yielding $i^3 = i^2 \cdot i = (-1)\cdot i = -i$
            and $i^4 = (i^2)^2 = (-1)^2 = 1$. 
            Multiply  $\frac 1i = -i$ with the
            denominator in to verify this equation.
            From $i^4 = 1$ follows
		$$
                i^{17} = i^{4 \cdot 4 + 1} = (i^4)^4 \cdot i = 1^4 \cdot i = i,
                $$
            i.e. all equations are correct.
}
